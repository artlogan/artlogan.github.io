  • You will not get good at something unless you spend a very large amount of focused time doing it.
  • You will not develop a sense of fluency without not weaning oneself off crutches like spellcheck, copilots, etc.
  • It often happens that there is a prevailing bias pervading all thinking in a certain time or place, and there stating the opposite is curative because it is a counterbalance pushing the whole situation away from extremism, which is usually wrong. It is then written as if it were true accross time, absolutely. However: it is not, and if in subsequent generations it inspires zealotry it creates structurally precisely the same problem it was trying to solve.
